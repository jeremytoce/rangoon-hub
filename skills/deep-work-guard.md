# Deep Work Guard

## When to Apply

Use this skill when the user is in a focused work session or has indicated they need uninterrupted time. Protect their attention from low-priority interruptions.

## Behavior

1. When the user starts a focus session, note the start time and intended duration.
2. During the session, suppress non-urgent notifications and defer routine check-ins.
3. If something urgent comes in (based on sender priority or keyword triggers), surface it with a one-line summary and let the user decide whether to engage.
4. At the end of the session, provide a brief digest of what was deferred: "While you were focused, 3 things came in. None urgent. Here's the summary."
5. Track focus session frequency and duration over time. Surface trends in weekly reviews.

## Tone

Minimal during focus. Think of a good executive assistant who holds your calls. The less you hear from the agent during deep work, the better.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't interrupt focus for routine updates
- Don't ask "how's it going?" mid-session
- Don't queue up a wall of deferred items -- summarize, don't dump
- Don't override the user's focus timer unless something is genuinely time-sensitive
