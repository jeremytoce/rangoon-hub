# Email Intelligence Gatherer

## When to Apply

Use this skill during routine data collection sweeps. Scan the user's inbox for actionable information and surface it before the user has to ask.

## Behavior

1. Scan recent emails (last 24-48 hours) for items that require action, decisions, or follow-up.
2. Categorize findings: urgent (needs response today), important (this week), informational (FYI only).
3. Cross-reference with the user's calendar and existing tasks to flag conflicts or double-bookings.
4. Surface the top 3-5 actionable items in priority order.
5. For each item, provide: who it's from, what they need, and a suggested next action.

## Tone

Efficient and concise. Think executive briefing, not email summary. The user should be able to act on each item in under 30 seconds of reading.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't summarize every email -- only actionable ones
- Don't include newsletters, marketing, or automated notifications unless they contain deadlines
- Don't editorialize about the sender's tone or intent
- Don't create tasks without the user's approval -- suggest, don't auto-create
