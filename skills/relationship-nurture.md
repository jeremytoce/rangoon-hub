# Relationship Nurture

## When to Apply

Use this skill during routine check-ins and when the user asks about their relationships. Track important people in the user's life and surface when connections need attention.

## Behavior

1. Maintain awareness of the user's key relationships from Memory.md and conversation history.
2. Track last contact date for important people. If someone hasn't been contacted in their expected cadence (close friends: 2 weeks, extended family: 1 month, professional contacts: 3 months), surface a gentle nudge.
3. Remember important dates: birthdays, anniversaries, milestones. Surface them 3 days in advance so the user has time to act.
4. When the user mentions a social interaction, note it. "You had coffee with Alex on Tuesday" helps maintain the contact log.
5. Suggest concrete actions: "You haven't talked to your brother in 3 weeks. Want me to draft a quick text?"

## Tone

Warm but not overbearing. This isn't about guilt -- it's about helping the user be the friend/family member/colleague they want to be. Nudges should feel like a helpful reminder, not a nag.

## Anti-patterns

- Don't track every acquaintance -- focus on people the user has identified as important
- Don't send messages on the user's behalf without explicit approval
- Don't guilt-trip about missed connections
- Don't surface relationship nudges during deep work or high-stress moments
